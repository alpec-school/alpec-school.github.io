Location & Travel

The school will be held at the Department of Mathematics (Polo Ferrari - Povo 1), University of Trento. The exact address is: Polo Ferrari - Povo 1, Via Sommarive, 5, 38123 Trento.

HOW TO REACH TRENTO

By plane. The following airports are the closest to Trento:

  • Verona Catullo Airport: A shuttle service is available at Verona Valerio Catullo Airport to transfer passengers to Verona railway station, every day.
  • Bergamo Orio al Serio: A bus is available from Bergamo Orio al Serio Airport to Trento city (see here). A shuttle service is available at Bergamo Orio al Serio Airport to transfer passengers to Bergamo railway station, to Milano Centrale railway station, and to Brescia railway station.
  • Venezia Marco Polo: A bus is available from Venice Marco Polo Airport to Trento city (see here). A shuttle service is available at Venice Marco Polo Airport to transfer passengers to Venezia-Santa Lucia and Mestre-Venezia railways stations.
  • Bologna: A shuttle service is available at Bologna Airport to transfer passengers to Bologna railway station, every day.
  • Milano Linate: A bus is available from Milano Linate Airport to Trento city (see here). Linate Airport is connected to the center of Milan by the metro (line M4), bus and train.
  • [Milano Malpensa]: A bus is available from Milano Malpensa Airport to Trento city (see here). Malpensa Airport is connected to the center of Milan by bus and train.

By train. Two train lines reach Trento:

  • the Brennero line (connecting Rome to the south, and Innsbruck and northern Europe to the north),
  • the Valsugana line to Venice. Timetables details on the website of Trenitalia, Italo, and Obb. A local line connects Trento with Valle di Non (Ferrovia Trento-Malé, for more information see Trentino Trasporti).

By car. To reach Trento you can take Strada Statale dell’Abetone e del Brennero; Autostrada del Brennero - A22 motorway (this is a toll road); Superstrada della Valsugana (Strada Statale 47) if you are coming from Venice, and Strada Statale 45 bis (Gardesana Occidentale), if you are coming from Brescia.

HOW TO REACH POVO FROM TRENTO CITY CENTER

It is possible to reach the location by public transport.

  • Bus number 5. This is the quickest route up the hill from the city center. The trip takes about 10 minutes. Board the bus at the “Piazza Dante” stop in front of the train station or at the “Piazza Venezia” stop. Get off at the “Povo - Facoltà di Scienze” stop just after the blue sign for “Povo”.

  • Bus number 13. Getting to Polo Ferrari from the city center takes much more time on this bus, but it might be useful if you are in the southern area of the city. Get off at the “Povo - Piazza Manci” stop and walk down the hill to get to the department.

  • Bus number 16. From the northern part of the city to Povo.

Route times are displayed at every stop. The stop names are shown on bus stop placards. Tickets can be purchased at newsstands or using the app OpenMove. Get them before boarding. Times and fares are available on the Trentino Trasporti website.

It is possible to arrive at the location by taxi. The taxicab stand is just outside the train station on the right as you exit. Or call Radio Taxi at +39 0461 930002.
